Steve Jobs

the co-founder and chief executive of Apple Computer

Here's a time line of Steve Jobs:

  • 1955 - Born in, San Francisco, California, U.S.
  • 1972 - Attended Reed College in 1972 before withdrawing that same year.
  • 1974 - Traveled through India seeking enlightenment and studying Zen Buddhism.
  • 1976 - Jobs and Wozniak co-founded Apple to sell Wozniak's Apple I personal computer
  • 1979 - Jobs saw the commercial potential of the Xerox Alto.
  • 1985 - Jobs was forced out of Apple after a long power struggle with the company's board and its then-CEO, John Sculley. Jobs found NeXT the same year.
  • 1986 - Jobs helped to develop the visual effects industry when he funded the computer graphics division of George Lucas's Lucasfilm.
  • 1997 - Jobs returned to Apple as CEO after the company's acquisition of NeXT, he was largely responsible for reviving Apple.
  • 2001 - Jobs Lunched the new Mac OS X (now known as macOS), which was originally Mac OS was replaced with the completely.
  • 2003 - Jobs was diagnosed with a pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor.
  • 2011 - He died of respiratory arrest related to the tumor on October 5, at the age of 56.
  • 2022 - He was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom.
